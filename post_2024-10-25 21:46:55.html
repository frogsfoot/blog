Letter #004


Another week gone by and i am making strides in being a human person. These letters become harder and harder to write as things become normal and lose their novelty. We had a final flatmate move in earlier this week, a month late. she moved from another accommodation building because the noise and poor hygiene was unbearable, so i cant imagine why she chose the accommodation infamous for our silverfish. I've only seen 6 so far, two in the bathroom, two on my desk (or maybe the same one a few days apart?) and two in other parts of the flat, but I now understand, with clarity, why they were once the most annoying mob in minecraft. the little blighters are fast. Other than that, our flat is actually alright. no mould, no weird smells or stains, warm most of the time, it's almost idyllic. My immune system seems to have increased tenfold since moving out of our cold and damp house in the middle of nowhere. who knew that being perpetually in 10°C (50°F) year round wouldn't be good for you? I've never actually felt my radiator in my room work, but sometimes i have to open my windows at night because it's too hot. Whether that's because of the radiators or the fact that we're on the top floor is yet to be decided, but it's a welcome quirk as we head into the winter months.

Halloween celebrations are in full swing. I'd love to attach photos of all the events but unfortunately the internet is the internet, and i dont need some strange creature from the depths knocking on my door at 3am demanding feet pics or whatever. My flatmates and I carved pumpkins, and it turns out a few had never carved before! it was really fun bonding over that, if i figured out how to, i've attached an appropriately dithered photo of my pumpkin, if not, imagine a big ol' open smiley face. :D <- style. Halloween at university is tied hand in hand with partying, and i've done more than i ever thought i would. and i've missed every single drama soc social except one. and that one was [as i write this] yesterday, and i stayed for approximately an hour before leaving to go watch hot fuzz with the techies. it was wonderful! it was less wonderful to wake up this morning for a shift at My Job, but i guess it's all swings and roundabouts, really. I'm in rehearsals 3 times a week, and so far they've all ended with a trip to the SU for drinks. drinking with theatre kids is a dangerous game, but it can also be a hoot. The show itself is going great! everyone is so so so lovely and i ended up sat on the concrete outside for hours talking to one of my castmates about art and storytelling. that's an insanely niche thing to be able to talk about for multiple hours! it's going so well, in fact, that i'm currently in the process of applying to co-direct this year's shakespeare! and it looks like i've got a pretty good shot, too?]

Speaking of work, i have a 9am start tomorrow with a 1am finish! by god i wish that were a joke. today's shift had 3 hours of unplanned overtime, so who knows WHAT tomorrow will bring. see you on the flipside :)


[I will, for the record, state that i am off the clock from 3pm to 10pm, so it's not actually that horrific, i'll just take a good nap.]
